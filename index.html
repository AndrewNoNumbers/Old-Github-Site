  • I'm Andrew Sierran Chen. I spend a lot of time conceptualizing, designing, and testing technology products. I previously interned in Beijing, talked to President Obama, tested the Cr-48 for Google, started two startups, and lived in a van. I now excitedly work on TinyTerms, using design to build a better way of writing and reading legal contracts. If you're interested in society, law, or design, you should talk to me. Besides design, I like to keep myself current on psychology (particularly cognition & memory, behavior, and personality), societal issues, education issues, sustainability, and Kanye West. I am a maker, hacker, philosopher, and critical thinker. My latest project is TinyTerms, which was born out of Startup Weekend Legaltech and won Github's Judge's Choice award. I post occasionally provocative things to Twitter and infrequent photos to Flickr and Instagram.
